FINANCIAL MANAGER

Financial managers are responsible for managing how a company spends and saves its finances. They make some of the biggest decisions that affect how a company moves forward.

Education and Training

To be a financial manager, you need to have at least a bachelor's degree. You can study a subject like finance or business. Or, you can take economics or accounting. It takes 4 years to earn a bachelor's degree.
Many managers hold a master's degree as well. Relevant programs include business administration, finance, and economics. A master's degree is required for many positions. It will also give you more chances to advance in the career. You can earn a master's degree in 1 to 2 years.

Earnings

This is not a field you can enter straight out of school. People need to start in an entry-level position and work their way up to a manager role. Starting salaries for many finance professionals range from about $44,000 to $65,000 a year.
Once established, financial managers can earn between $68,000 and $200,000 a year or more. People who rise to the highest positions earn the most. CFOs and vice presidents of finance can make from $100,000 to $300,000 a year. Those at big companies may earn even more.

Core Tasks

  • Manage the departments in charge of company funds
  • Oversee expense tracking and budgeting
  • Analyze financial data to identify economic trends
  • Prepare financial statements and cost-benefit analyses
  • Act as liaisons between company and investors

Workplace Conditions

  • Work indoors in offices
  • 40 to 60 hours a week, depending on deadlines
  • Sitting at a desk for long periods may cause back, neck, or eye strain
  • May travel to meet with clients
  • Often talk to clients, managers, accountants, and bookkeepers
  • Making big financial decisions can be stressful
